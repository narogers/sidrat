Virgin New Adventures

Once Doctor Who went off the air in 1989 it took only two years for Virgin publishing to pick up the torch. More edgy and adult oriented than the television series it allowed for 'stories too big for the small screen'. The line continued until May 1997 when the BBC took over in expectation of a revival following a made-for-TV movie.

Minus the Doctor the series continued with Bernice Summerfield as its main protagonist although interest waned over time. Eventually the New Adventures came to an end in 1999 with the release of Twilight of the Gods.
