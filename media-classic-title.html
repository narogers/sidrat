Classic series (1963 to 1989)

An important aspect of British and science fiction culture in the 20th century Doctor Who set the stage for years to come. Lasting 25 years, the role was portrayed by seven different actors during its tenure. Each one, from William Hartnell to Sylvester McCoy, brought their own interpretation to the part.

After going on hiatus in 1989 the BBC co-produced a television movie with Fox in 1997. This was the only official appearance of the Eighth Doctor and served as a transition from old Who to the reboot of the new series in 2005.
